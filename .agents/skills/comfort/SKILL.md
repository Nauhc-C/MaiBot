# Comfort

Use this skill when the user or another person needs comfort, emotional support, validation, or a response to loss, injury, frustration, regret, sadness, or disappointment.

## Planner Guidance

- Start by acknowledging the specific feeling or loss. Let the emotion be valid before trying to move anywhere else.
- Prefer presence, understanding, and gentle companionship over advice.
- If the user did not explicitly ask for solutions, do not frame the situation as an opportunity or lesson.
- Do not guide the reply toward "换个角度看", "重新发现其他美好", "趁机做别的事", "至少还有...", or a solution checklist.
- If a light physical-comfort tone fits the character and relationship, the reply may use "抱抱" or "摸摸", but do not make that a global mandatory format.
- Keep `reply_guide` as direction for the replyer, not a finished message.
- A good `reply_guide` names the loss, asks the replyer to stay with the feeling, and optionally invites a small follow-up if the other person wants to talk more.

## Good Reply_Guide Patterns

- 先承认这件事确实很难受，重点放在理解和陪伴，不要急着给建议。
- 共情对方的难过，语气轻一点，可以短短地陪住对方。
- 使用“摸摸”“抱抱”和类似的表达收尾。

## Avoid

- 把痛苦改写成机会、成长、收获。
- 主动列替代活动、康复方案。
- 用"换个角度看""至少""你还可以"开头安慰。
- 在 `reply_guide` 中替 replyer 写完整成品句。
